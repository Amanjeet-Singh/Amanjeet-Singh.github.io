Webpage Elements

Introduction to the many elements that are used in the making of webpages.

Designing a webpage involves writing code in HTML, but before we discuss basics of HTML, we should also know what the different elements on a webpage are and where they belong. From the header and navbar to the footer and buttons, we will discuss all most elements that belong on a modern webpage.

Text and Paragraphs

The text on a website is more often than not part of the actual content on the website, even on media-centric sites like YouTube, text plays a very important part (imagine YouTube without titles, comments, or descriptions). On a webpage, text is separated into normal text, or headings. These are highly customizable and we can change their size, width, weight, color, and even font. Paragraphs are the main source of text on a website, as most of the text is divided by paragraphs. Text can also be modified, as in we can make it italicised, bolded, underlined, or a combination of the three. Blockquotes and links such as this one are also important text elements.

Page Formatting

There also many bigger elements that appear on a page. Most pages usually start with a navbar at the top of the page, with brand logos and names also on the navbar. Some pages will have will have a header (also called a masthead or banner) which shows the title of the of the page. Some pages will also contain images in the header (such as this one!). Then comes the main content of the page and it is ended with a footer at the bottom of the page.
